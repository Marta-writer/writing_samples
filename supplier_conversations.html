Supplier Conversations

Responding to emails from suppliers is a time-intensive task that often requires significant manual effort from your Accounts Payable (AP) team, which drains their productivity. Also, your AP team typically needs to handle numerous queries from internal employees. Supplier Conversations helps to handle these routine tasks and ease the burden on your AP team. It allows AP clerks to focus on processing invoices rather than dealing with queries.

Supplier Conversations provides AI assistance for responding to various supplier and internal user queries via email. Whether they inquire about a specific invoice, seek information on payment status, or need support for any invoicing-related issues, the Supplier Conversations AI assistant is ready to help. Its responses include trusted CC recipients and email attachments.

Since the AI assistant is hosted on a highly secure platform, you can rest assured that your data is safe.

To configure company and AI assistant settings for Supplier Conversations, go to the Supplier Conversations administration page.

Manage supplier contacts¶

To manage email addresses or domains assigned to suppliers for Supplier Conversations, go to Supplier > Enterprise > Contact persons.

How the AI assistant process works¶

The AI assistant can receive invoice inquiry emails from both known and unknown supplier contacts. To determine whether an email is from a known or an unknown contact, the following steps are carried out:

  1. The AI assistant tries to find a supplier contact in Medius with an email address that matches the sender’s.

  2. If an exact match is found, the email is from a known contact.

  3. Conversely, if an exact match isn’t found, the email is from an unknown contact.

The remainder of the process then differs between a known contact and an unknown contact.

Known contact¶

For a known contact, the supplier has been identified at this point. Therefore, the AI assistant may be able to respond directly to the sender, without involving your AP team.

The process continues as follows:

  1. The AI assistant extracts the invoice number from the email.

  2. The AI assistant verifies that the invoice exists in Medius and is from the expected supplier.

  3. If a valid invoice is found, the AI assistant emails an appropriate response to the sender, and the process ends.

  4. If a valid invoice isn’t found, the AI assistant removes special characters from the extracted invoice number and checks again.

    Note

    Examples of special characters that are removed include: . , ; : - ' " ! ? _ / # * + & % ( ) [ ] £ $ € ¥, and the space character.

  5. If a valid invoice is found at the second attempt, the AI assistant emails an appropriate response to the sender, and the process ends.

  6. If a valid invoice still isn’t found in Medius:

    • If an invoice is attached to the email:

      1. The AI assistant forwards the inquiry email, including the attached invoice, to your AP team, along with an advisory message stating that no matching invoice in Medius was found.

      2. The AI assistant notifies the sender that the invoice wasn’t found, and that the inquiry has been forwarded to your AP team.

    • Otherwise, if an invoice isn’t attached, the AI assistant notifies the sender that the invoice wasn’t found in Medius, with instructions to send another email—with an invoice attached—directly to your AP team.

Unknown contact¶

For an unknown contact, a supplier hasn’t been identified at this point. Consequently, the AI assistant won’t respond directly to the sender and may escalate the inquiry to your AP team.

The process continues as follows:

  1. The AI assistant extracts the invoice number from the email.

  2. The AI assistant generates a list of possible suppliers based on the extracted invoice number.

  3. If no suppliers are found in Medius, the AI assistant removes special characters from the extracted invoice number and regenerates the list of possible suppliers.

    Note

    Examples of special characters that are removed include: . , ; : - ' " ! ? _ / # * + & % ( ) [ ] £ $ € ¥, and the space character.

  4. The AI assistant carries out further checks in an attempt to reduce the list to a single supplier. This involves extracting and inspecting data across the following elements of the email:

    • Sender email address and display name

    • Subject line

    • Email body

    • Email signature

    • Names and content of file attachments

    The AI assistant tries to find a match between all extracted data and the names of suppliers held in Medius.

  5. Matching suppliers are retained in the list of possible suppliers, others are discarded.

  6. At this point, there are three possible outcomes:

    1. No suppliers are identified – see No supplier matches.

    2. A single supplier is identified – see Single supplier match.

    3. Multiple possible suppliers remain – see Multiple supplier matches.

Tip

To test how the AI assistant handles unknown contacts, email an inquiry from an unknown domain. You can include the supplier’s name in the email signature. Avoid using your actual company name and logo. For example, you could use a personal email account at @gmail.com, or clearly state the supplier name related to the invoice.

No supplier matches¶

In this case, there are two possible courses of action:

  1. For an urgent email: the AI assistant forwards the sender’s inquiry email to your AP team, along with an advisory message stating that no matching supplier was found.

    Note

    Examples where an email is deemed urgent include:

    • Requests for changes to banking details

    • Trading termination threats due to late payment

    • Invoice disputes

    • Credit hold/shut-off notices

  2. For a non-urgent email: the AI assistant disregards the sender’s inquiry.

Single supplier match¶

In this case, your AP team receives a notification email containing:

  • The suggested supplier name.

  • Two approval options:

    1. Approve the specific email address – this adds the contact to the supplier’s record in Medius, and the unknown contact becomes a known contact. The AI assistant then processes the current invoice inquiry and sends an appropriate response to the sender.

    2. Approve the email domain – this adds the entire email domain—for example, @medius.com—to the contact list of the supplier’s record in Medius. The AI assistant will subsequently treat senders of emails from this domain as known contacts and automatically process their invoice inquiries.

Note

To approve an unknown contact via email, a user must have a role with the Suite Services.Generic.ManageSupplierConversations access key switched on.

This access key is switched on by default for the Admin role. However, other users can also perform approval if they have been granted this access key.

Attention

For security reasons, generic public domains, such as @gmail.com or @hotmail.com, are blocked. This keeps supplier invoice data secure and prevents unauthorized access. For blocked domains, the Approve Domain option is unavailable.

Multiple supplier matches¶

If the AI assistant matches the unknown contact to more than one supplier, your AP team receives a notification email containing a list of suggested supplier names.

Note

To approve an unknown contact via email, a user must have a role with the Suite Services.Generic.ManageSupplierConversations access key switched on.

This access key is switched on by default for the Admin role. However, other users can also perform approval if they have been granted this access key.

After a member of your AP team selects a supplier from the list, the contact is added to that supplier’s record in Medius. The AI assistant then processes the current invoice inquiry and sends an appropriate response to the sender.

Duplicate supplier contact approval¶

Occasionally, a member of the AP team may try to approve an unknown email address or domain that another user has already approved. In this case, the duplicate approval attempt is stopped, and the user is directed to a special page within the Medius system that indicates the issue.

Handle escalated emails¶

The Supplier Conversations AI bot escalates emails to the AP team when it cannot automatically resolve a query or requires human verification.

These escalations usually happen in the following situations:

  • The bot recognizes the supplier but can’t find the referenced invoice in Medius.

  • The bot cannot match the sender to any existing supplier in Medius, and the enquiry implies urgency.

  • The sender is unknown at first, but the bot finds information such as subject lines or email signatures that link them to suppliers in Medius. Then the bot refers to the AP team for confirmation.

Caution notice¶

When an email comes in, the bot uses standard security checks to see if the sender can be trusted.

If an email cannot be fully verified, it may still be delivered, but it could pose a potential security risk. This means the email might not actually be from the sender it appears to be from.

A caution notice appears at the top of these emails when they are sent to the AP team. This warning is there to remind you to be careful before replying or acting on the email.

Priority labels¶

An escalated email is tagged with one of the following priority labels: High, Medium, or Low. This helps AP users quickly spot and sort issues by importance.

The priority shows at the start of the subject line. For example:

“High Priority - Supplier Inquiry Escalation - {Original subject text}”

Email categories¶

Every email shows its category based on the reason for escalation. Categories include:

  • Overdue Invoice

  • Payment Reminder

  • Supplier Onboarding

  • Others – used when the reason for escalation doesn’t fit any specific category. Emails in this group are always treated as low priority.

These categories are easy to see at the top of each email, above the greeting. If there is more than one reason for escalation, the categories are listed by priority.

Redirect emails to the AI assistant automatically¶

You can automatically redirect emails to the Supplier Conversations AI assistant so you no longer have to manually share the Supplier Conversations email address with suppliers or redirect emails to the AI assistant yourself.

Email redirect rule¶

This rule will automatically send emails to the Supplier Conversations AI assistant for processing.

Prerequisite¶

To set an email redirect rule, you need to have a Microsoft 365 Global Administrator account.

How to set an email redirect rule¶

  1. Login to Microsoft 365 Admin Portal.

  2. On the Admin Centers, click Exchange.

  3. In the left navigation pane, go to Mail Flow and select Rules.

  4. Click Add a rule, and then Create a new rule.

  5. Fill in the required fields. In the Name field, type “Medius Supplier Conversations”.

  6. Under Apply this rule if, select the recipient and is this person in the two drop-down lists.

  7. Click the + icon and enter your group mailbox address, for example: apteam@customername.com.

  8. Under Do the following, select Redirect the message to and these recipients.

  9. Enter the email address provided by Medius: customername@agent.medius.com.

  10. If you want to keep a copy of emails, set up an additional mailbox to which emails will be redirected. In step 8, you can configure the rule to redirect emails to multiple mailboxes within the same rule.

    Example:

    • Mailbox: ap@tenant.com

    • Redirect rule – redirects to: tenant@agent.medius.com, ap-copy@tenant.com

  11. Click Save or Next to continue to the next section.

  12. On the Set Rule Settings:

    • Set the Rule mode to Enforce.

    • Set the severity to Low.

    • Do not add any dates in the date range.

    • Set the Match sender address in Message to Header.

  13. Review the settings and click Finish to create the rule. Note that it will be disabled by default.

  14. On the list of rules, click the Medius Supplier Conversations rule and switch the toggle to enable it.

  15. Wait for confirmation and close the settings.

  16. The rule will now be enabled.

Email redirection vs forwarding¶

Supplier Conversations requires emails to be redirected rather than forwarded to ensure accurate processing.

Email Redirection¶

Emails are rerouted directly to the AI assistant as soon as they reach your email exchange server. The original sender’s information is retained, which allows the AI assistant to recognize the email as coming from the supplier and ensure accurate processing.

Email Forwarding¶

Emails first land in your AP inbox before being sent to the AI assistant. This causes the AI assistant to misinterpret the sender as your AP team instead of the supplier, which disrupts workflows and may lead to errors.

DKIM Validation¶

When setting up the auto-redirect rule in the Microsoft Exchange Server, we need your IT team to modify a setting related to external senders.

It’s common to include an External Sender disclaimer in emails, such as:

CAUTION: This email originated from outside of [organization]. Do not click links or open attachments unless you know this sender and are expecting this content.

When an External Sender disclaimer is added by the Microsoft Exchange Server before emails are redirected to Supplier Conversations, it modifies the email content. This alteration causes the DKIM validation process to fail. DKIM is a method used to verify that an email has not been tampered with in transit, ensuring its authenticity. Therefore, when the content of the email is changed by adding the external sender disclaimer, the DKIM signature no longer matches, and the validation fails. As a result, emails aren’t processed due to potential security risks.

To resolve this issue, disable the Microsoft Exchange Server Mail Flow rules that add the External Sender disclaimer to all external emails. This setting is responsible for appending the disclaimer that triggers the DKIM failure. The adjustment should only apply to the group mailbox that receives emails from suppliers and redirects them to the Supplier Conversations AI assistant for processing. By disabling this setting for that specific mailbox, the emails can be redirected without modification, ensuring that the DKIM validation is successful, and the emails are correctly processed by Supplier Conversations.

How to disable Mail Flow rules for External Sender disclaimer¶

  1. Login to Microsoft 365 Admin Portal.

  2. On the Admin Centers, click on Exchange.

  3. In the left navigation pane, go to Mail Flow and select Rules.

  4. Select the rule that is applying the warning banner.

  5. Click the Except If rule drop-down list and select Is Sent To.

  6. Enter the email address of the group mailbox, for example: apteam@customername.com.

  7. Click the + icon and add another condition.

  8. Select Is Received From and enter the Medius email address: customername@agent.medius.com.

  9. Ensure the condition between these rules is set to Or.

  10. Click Save or Next.

Internal user queries¶

To reduce the manual workload of your AP team, the AI assistant handles internal users’ queries alongside inquiries from supplier contacts.

The AI assistant provides internal users with invoice details based on their access permissions to the given invoice. If users don’t have access, then the relevant supplier and company names are shared with them.
