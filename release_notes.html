New – Redesigned user experience and navigation¶

Description:

This release brings a modern user experience with updated navigation, better search, and new personalized home pages for AP and Approver roles. It also features a redesigned Medius AP Automation inbox to help you manage tasks with ease.

These improvements make the interface more intuitive, so you can navigate the system more easily and focus on your most important tasks.

Change – Daily reminders for unhandled documents¶

Description:

We are about to update how reminder notifications work to ensure a more consistent experience for all customers and invoice types.

Reminders for documents will be triggered daily by a scheduled job. User-level settings will continue to determine when reminders are delivered to the user.

This change does not affect direct notifications sent when a document is first delivered to a user. Only reminder notifications are impacted.

Availability:

This change will be introduced between January and March of the upcoming year.

Capture¶

New – Recognizing buyers and suppliers using order numbers¶

Description:

Medius Capture can now recognize the buyer and supplier on PO invoices by using the related order number, even for first-time invoices from new suppliers.

This release also adds a new Company and supplier matching pop-up window, which you can open by clicking the information icon next to the PO number field. This window explains how the system matched the company and supplier, shows the values found on the PO, and lists any automated actions, such as validating the company or warning about a supplier mismatch.

This update reduces manual work and helps you quickly fix any differences between invoice and PO data.

For more information, see the documentation.

Availability:

This functionality will be made available to all customers gradually during January 2026. No system configuration is necessary.

Supplier Conversations¶

New – Caution notice for unverified emails¶

Description:

To improve safety, a caution notice now appears at the top of unverified emails escalated to the Accounts Payable (AP) team by the AI assistant. This notice reminds recipients to consider security implications before replying to or acting on such emails. Unverified means that the sender’s identity couldn’t be authenticated.

The aim is to help AP users make informed decisions and handle potentially malicious emails with extra care.

For more information, see the documentation.

Availability:

This functionality is available to all users by default. No system configuration is necessary.

New – Improvements to escalated emails¶

Description:

To help the Accounts Payable (AP) team work more efficiently and attend to urgent tasks first, we’ve made several improvements to email escalation:

  • Priority labelling – an escalated email is now tagged with one of the following priority labels: High, Medium, or Low. This helps AP users to quickly identify and prioritize issues.

    The priority appears at the beginning of the subject line. For example:

    “High Priority - Supplier Inquiry Escalation - {ORIGINAL_SUBJECT_TEXT_FROM_SENDERS_EMAIL}”

  • Email categorization – an escalated email now shows a category based on the reason for escalation. Categories include:

    • Overdue Invoice

    • Payment Reminder

    • Supplier Onboarding

    • Others – used where the reason for escalation isn’t specifically categorized. An escalated email in this category is always treated as low priority.

    The category is shown at the top of each email, above the greeting. If the reason for escalation falls within more than one category, the applicable categories are listed in priority order.

For more information, see the documentation.

Availability:

This functionality is available to all users by default. No system configuration is necessary.
